The Critical Research Gap

No UV-VOC Studies

Despite extensive research on 3D printing emissions, no studies have examined how UV radiation affects VOC emissions from finished printed objects.

Indoor Air Quality

3D printed objects in homes and schools may emit harmful VOCs when exposed to sunlight through windows.

Environmental Impact

Ocean plastic pollution may be exacerbated by UV-induced chemical emissions from 3D printed waste.

Research Methodology

1

Sample Preparation

Create standardized 3D printed specimens using PLA, ABS, and PETG filaments with controlled printing parameters.

FDM Printing Standardized Specimens
2

UV Exposure Protocol

Systematically expose samples to controlled UV radiation (365nm) for varying durations (0-48 hours) under constant environmental conditions.

365nm Wavelength Controlled Environment
3

VOC Analysis

Use gas chromatography-mass spectrometry (GC-MS) to identify and quantify VOC emissions from UV-exposed samples.

GC-MS Analysis Quantitative Results
4

Material Characterization

Analyze physical and chemical changes using FTIR spectroscopy, SEM imaging, and mechanical testing.

FTIR Spectroscopy SEM Imaging
